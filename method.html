Survey design Method and Conclusion

Creating and distributing the survey

In summary, the aims of this survey were to understand what accessibility features currently players use, how useful do players think the current design of the game is in terms of accessibility and what could be changed to improve accessibility. I believe this survey was successful in answering those aims. This survey found that the majority of players use gyro and little else especially if those accessibility options aren’t already included in the game (e.g., mods).

The survey respondent’s satisfaction with the current accessibility design varied, with gyro and menu/text design being widely agreed to be accessible and comfortable. With controller buttons and special design (Splattercolour Screen) having mixed opinions. Finally, the consensus on stick controls and audio was that they lacked options to change them if they were uncomfortable.

Lastly, the main takeaway from section 3 is that regardless of the satisfaction of certain features or settings currently in the game, (in response to the questions in section 2) a large number of players wanted more settings added. Less than 7% of people surveyed thought no changes were needed for stick controls, controller settings, audio and visual design. Gyro had the most players that didn’t want any changes made but that was only 18%. This leads me to conclude that while the design of Splatoon 3 in terms of accessibility is good with most players not having issues with various aspects of the game however, the lack of settings makes it difficult to play comfortably if you do have issues and makes it very rigid if you want to customise your experience. /p>

Strengths and weaknesses of this survey

The process of creating a survey and writing a report on the findings has been a useful experience. I believe that I have done a good job of planning, surveying and analysing the data. I think that my timeline provided me with a useful schedule to complete the actions needed to create, analyse and report on my survey. However, there are still aspects of this process I would improve when carrying out another survey in the future. The main points for improvement are question design/misinterpretation and biased sampling.

The biggest challenge with designing this survey for me was creating questions which were clear and left little room for misinterpretation. While overall I think I did a good job on this but, there were still some definitions and questions which were lacking. For example, stick controls weren’t defined as I had assumed people would use the same definition as the game. When I referred to button mapping and language settings I didn’t specify if it was the current features of the Nintendo Switch console or the hypothetical Splatoon 3 in game settings, this led to some confusion. In future I would allocate more time to piloting my survey with a larger group of people to get feedback before I fully send out my survey (for reference I sent my survey to two people to check).

One concern I had when receiving responses for my survey was the possibility of biased sampling. I believe this could be an issue because of where I posted the survey. The survey was posted to Discord severs, Blue Sky and Twitter (X) from my own accounts. There are some possible issues with bias, these are a geographical/language barrier bias and demographic bias (competitive/casual bias). The language/geographic bias is partly caused by the survey only being available in English meaning anyone who is not fluent enough in reading or writing in English will be unable to complete the survey. This leaves out a large proportion of non-European and non-American countries (South America, Asia, Africa and people in European countries who don’t speak English) resulting in a “Western” bias. I didn’t include a question asking about a respondent’s region which would have helped given me further insight into the geographical distribution of the respondents and helped me understand the possible bias in my survey. This is also the same idea with the competitive/casual demographic, when sending the survey out to be filled I sent it to friends and people I know which are mostly competitive players. The amount of time a competitive player has played Splatoon 3, and their views of the game will be different to a player who has 10hrs in a game. This can cause a skew and possible bias in the results. Next time I should consider possible sources of bias that could affect my survey and account for them. I should also add questions to the survey to better understand the demographic of the survey respondents such as “What country do you live in?” or “Do you consider yourself a competitive or casual player?”.
