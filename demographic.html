Demographic of the participants in the survey

Age Groups

My survey shows that 84% of participants are under the age of 25 which can be broken down into 24% under 18, 36% between 22 and 25 and 24% between 26 and 29. The next 12% are aged between 26 and 37. This is mostly what I expected the age demographic to be since this game is typically advertised to a younger audience.

Hours Played per Age Group per Week

The graph above shows that 42% of people play Splatoon 3 3-5 times a week, 25% 0-2 times, 23% 6-8 times and 10% of people playing over 8 hours per week. This shows that the survey demographic frequently plays Splatoon 3, and a decent proportion play on average daily.

Settings Used

This question allows multiple answers to be selected so the percentages are shown in the graph below. The main data to focus on is the gyro and motion controls since these are the only 2 control types. The graph shows that 90% (162) of people use gyro (motion) controls since gyro and stick controls can’t be active at the same time, this should mean that 10% of players use stick controls. This is not reflected by the data. This could be because there are players who use both types of controls or, I wasn’t specific enough when phrasing the question. Gyro controls have the option to enable/disable the right stick on the controller for horizontal camera movement so, the question could be vague and cause a player who uses gyro controls with the right stick for horizontal camera movement to select both “Gyro aiming” and “Stick Controls”. While this data may not be fully accurate it does show that a majority of players use gyro controls.

Colour lock sets the colours used in the game to pre-set colours that are unable to change from match to match. The intended use for this is to make it easier to differentiate between their team’s colour and the opponent team’s colour. 12.7% of players surveyed used colour lock so, most players in the survey are okay with changing colours between matches and the colours themselves.

Button remapping allows the re-binding of an action to a different button. This was used by 6.1% of players which suggests that the majority of players are comfortable with the default control scheme.

External hardware and external software both have little use by participants in the survey (0% and 2.2% respectively). I believe this could be due to lack of knowledge about these options existing as well as Nintendo being strict on the modification of their software and hardware which can result in a permanent ban from online play making the risk not worth it. However, this cannot be proven with data from this survey and follow up questions would need to be asked.

The “Other” option was selected by 3.9% of people surveyed. These statements expanded on what types of external hardware was used (e.g., 3rd party controllers) or what situations they used settings like colour lock or button remapping in.
