Visual Accessibility Design in Splatoon 3

Visuals likert

This question asked the participant to agree or disagree with statements relating to the accessibility of the visual design in Splatoon 3.

In this section the statements with the largest percentage of agreement are text font readability, text size, colour lock options, brightness of the game, menu readability and navigation and language options. Each statement had the following total agreement percentage: 98.3%, 91.3%, 77.5%, 86.8%, 97.1%, 89.6%, 77.7% respectively. This shows the majority of people in the survey are satisfied with menu design (which to an extent does include text size and font) and suggests that it is designed well. Although not everyone agrees with these statements and out of the people who agreed, most only agree and not strongly agreed.

There were also statements which most people disagreed with, these were statements asking about aspects of visual design (effects, flashing lights, screen shaking) causing discomfort. This shows most people do not have issues with how the game handles these effects however, about 20% - 30% of participants said that it did cause them problems. This is still a decent proportion of the survey population that are affected by the design of the game.

The most divisive statement was if Splattercolour Screen (a special weapon in the game which puts up a large screen that causes high levels of colour desaturation, decreased brightness as well as plays a buzzing audio noise when the opposing team passes through the screen) caused visual discomfort. This was split with 51.7% agreeing with the statement and 48.3% and a relatively even split between each of the options (from 9.8% to 25.3% with most around 15%). This is a large number of players that are negatively affected by a single item in the game especially considering the developers have tried to change it previously to cause less discomfort.

Visuals Improvements

The percentage for answers varied greater compared to the other questions in this section, ranging from 12.1% - 67.2% (excluding no changes and other). The most popular options were “Increased colour lock options” (67.2%) then “Customize colour combinations” (61.1%). The least popular options were “Camera field of view slider” and “Button cooldown option when progressing text” with 12.1% and 12.2% respectively (see appendix A, A12). I believe increased colour options and colour customisation might not be linked to accessibility for some people, this is because it is a commonly requested aesthetic feature that has function beyond accessibility. Understanding why players chose certain features/settings would need to be looked into in future survey.

The “Other” option was selected by 5.6% of people and included suggestions such as having a setting to remove bright flashes, colour blind filters and disable other in game particle effect (such as warping, sparks, flashes).
