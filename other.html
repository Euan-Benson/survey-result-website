Other feedback and imporvements

Other Improvemants to the Accessibility

This question asked if there were any other ways to improve accessibility options outside what was already suggested in the previous questions. A few responses expanded on suggestions mentioned in previous questions such as turbo buttons options for weapons outside which weren’t mentioned in question 11, more detailed ideas about Splattercolour Screen and other suggestions about button remapping.

There were a few comments about supporting more types of controllers such as keyboard and mouse or a controller designed specifically for accessibility (similar to the Xbox adaptive controller). While an adaptive controller is a good method to improve accessibility to make it easier for players with physical or motor-control based disabilities. I classified them as outside of the scope of this survey since they aren’t directly a part of the video game. Lastly, a setting to change the aiming crosshair was mentioned which I thought was worth highlighting. Changing the size, shape and colour of the crosshair can help with visibility to make it more accessible.

To summarise, there are a range of accessibility options players would like included that were not mentioned in the survey. Being able to survey your player base for their thoughts is important so that there is a better understanding of what players want and how suggestions can be incorporated into your game so that can be improved.

Survey Feedback

I asked respondents for any feedback on how I conducted my survey to see if there was anything I could improve. The main comment was to include a neutral/not sure option for the likert questions, most responses said that they didn’t know about or didn’t have an opinion about some of the statements and a neutral option would help. When designing this survey, I decided not to include a neutral options because I didn’t want participants to sit on the fence. However, after reading the feedback some people didn’t feel knowledgeable enough to answer some statements in the likert questions meaning their selected answer did not reflect what they believed. This could lead to inaccurate data even if this was a small number of people who gave this feedback. Going forwards when designing another survey, I would include a “not sure” option for questions where the participants is not expected to know the answer/have an opinion (for example, question 6,7 and 8 in this survey).

Some people mentioned that some definitions were not clarified for example stick controls. There might be confusion because gyro controls also use stick controls for horizontal camera movement which could cause confusion. Other questions or definitions that caused confusion were question 3 (what counts as times per week) and people confusing button remapping/language settings already existing on the Nintendo Switch but not in Splatoon 3. How I would improve on this feedback in future will be addressed later on.
